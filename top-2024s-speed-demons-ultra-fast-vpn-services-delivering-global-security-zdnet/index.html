Top 2024’S Speed Demons: Ultra-Fast VPN Services Delivering Global Security | ZDNet

One question about VPNs I often encounter as a VPN reviewer is, which is the fastest VPN in the world? Though I have extensively tested , researched, and written about VPNs, there’s still no conclusive answer. A VPN can be relatively fast in one instance and slow in another, mainly influenced by factors like the encryption process, server load and distance, time of day, app configurations and settings, and more.

If you’re looking for the fastest VPN for browsing, remote work, streaming, gaming, or torrenting, we’ve run the speed tests and selected the best-performing services to help you out.

What is the fastest VPN right now?

Our pick for the fastest VPN overall is NordVPN, following speed tests in many locations. It works seamlessly on commonly-used devices like laptops and phones, with a rapidly growing global server fleet that makes it easier to find reliable servers in or near any location. The app updates very frequently, which can be annoying, but this is expected of such an elite service packed with many secure features.

Fastest VPN overall

NordVPN outshone rivals when we tested server connections in many regions, primarily North America and Europe. With an average 89% speed retention (11% reduction), it registered the least speed drops from the baseline 250Mbps internet connection. This great performance is partly due to the NordLynx protocol, an optimized version of the renowned WireGuard protocol used in most fast VPNs.

The extensive network of 6,400+ servers in 111 countries is another reason NordVPN tops the list of the fastest VPNs. You can easily find secure and fast servers in many more locations than what most competition offers. There are also specialty servers like double VPN, Onion over VPN, and obfuscated servers to facilitate smooth online activities. NordVPN is compatible with popular devices and platforms too, making it ZDNET’s pick for the best VPN overall and the best VPN for iPhone and iPad .

Will a VPN make my internet connection faster?

A VPN may slow down your internet connection, but can, in some cases, result in faster speeds. If you want to use a secure VPN and maintain your fast internet connection, opt for a reputable service that’s optimized for speed performance. Also, choose the nearest server location to shorten the distance your VPN traffic has to travel.

If your ISP (internet service provider) has capped speeds, leading to slower internet connections, then using a VPN can help to make the speeds faster. ISPs often do this if they see you’re using up a lot of bandwidth, for example, when downloading large files or streaming in high quality. Masking your IP address and encrypting your traffic means they can’t target or even see your activities, allowing you to browse at normal speeds.

Why do VPNs slow down internet speed?

When connected to a VPN, your data and internet traffic travels longer distances, leading to slower speeds. It routes through the VPN servers before reaching the websites you want to visit (and vice versa for the return journey). Also, data is encrypted and decrypted in the tunneling process, leading to slower speeds than unencrypted connections. Sometimes, the servers get overcrowded with many users, affecting the transmission speeds. You can always try different servers if you’re experiencing slow connections.

How we test VPNs

For this list of the fastest VPNs, we subjected 25+ reputable VPN services to a rigorous testing process. Each VPN’s download speeds were recorded for over two days in 10 different server locations. The VPN protocols were set to automatic, allowing each VPN to choose the best protocol to use with a particular server. Before each test, we did a full factory reset on the testing device. Whenever possible, we connected to servers in the following countries, or the closest server if the specific country wasn’t available:

  • US (North America)
  • Canada (North America)
  • Mexico (North America)
  • Brazil (South America)
  • UK (Europe)
  • Germany (Central Europe)
  • Japan (East Asia)
  • Netherlands (Western Europe)
  • Singapore (Southeast Asia)
  • Australia (Oceania)
  • Turkey (Europe/Asia)

Before each test, we measured baseline download speeds to calculate the speed retention or loss when using a VPN.

Ideally, if we ran these tests every month, we’d likely have a different order for the top 5 or 10 VPNs each time. But my best guess would be that the slowest VPNs would always be near the bottom and the fastest ones would consistently be near the top with this method.

FAQs about the fastest VPNs

What is the fastest free VPN and is it safe?

Windscribe is a fast, free, and safe VPN offering servers in many countries. Proton VPN is a close alternative with unlimited bandwidth for streaming, downloading, and sharing as much content as you want. These two free apps do not have ads and operate no-logs policies, meaning they won’t collect or share your information with third parties (like marketers).

How do I optimize my VPN speed?

For faster VPN speeds, connect via fast protocols like WireGuard and IKEv2 (or a proprietary one if offered). When using the OpenVPN protocol, try switching to TCP, which performs better than DCP. If your VPN supports AES 128-bit encryption, use this instead of the more advanced AES 256-bit. Features like split tunneling allow you to tunnel only specific and necessary apps on the VPN, saving you bandwidth.

Also, connect to servers closest to you for faster speeds. Use optimized servers for activities like streaming, gaming, or torrenting, if your VPN offers them. Additional things you can do to speed up VPN connections include disabling antivirus and firewalls on your device as well as rebooting your router if connections are slow.

Are VPN speed boosters a scam?

VPN speed boosters can’t give you speeds beyond limits set by your ISP. They’re only meant to improve efficiency in the VPN tunneling process so that you can get speeds almost as fast as your baseline connections. Therefore, you shouldn’t expect VPN speed booster features to offer advantages over speed limitations set by your internet service.

Surfshark vs NordVPN: Which VPN is fastest?

NordVPN is faster than Surfshark, especially when you use the in-house NordLynx protocol. It delivers better connections in several locations than Surfshark’s WireGuard protocol. However, we didn’t notice significant speed differences on OpenVPN and IKEv2/IPSec protocols over short and long distances. Both can reliably support smooth online streaming, gaming, and torrenting, and have custom settings to help you optimize speed performance.
